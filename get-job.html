Getting a CS Job at the End of History

personal reflections
2026-03-25 • status: in-progress

I want to kill myself all the time and being functional in the modern economy requires a certain level of just gritting your teeth and getting through it.

You need a job, I guess.

This will be useful for like another few months before Claude 5.5 or AGI or whatever. Just do it. You want to go to SF? Have a front row seat for the eschaton? You can't measure up to completing your stupid fucking classes at school? Well, oh well.

Basic Principles

The basic, essential elements you need to get a job, applicable to generally most everything:

  • Personal Hackathons:
    • Instead of "projects", think of portfolio building as competing in your own personal Hackathons. What wins a Hackathon overlaps substantially with what gets attention from employers.
    • Personal hackathon projects should be:
      • Impactful
      • Technically interesting
      • Original
      • Have a clear audience whose attention you want, and to whom this project is useful, interesting, exciting, and compelling to
      • What itch does it solve for them?
      • This audience, your metaphorical "judges" in your personal Hackathon, can be a community, person, subculture, really anything you want – you just need to hook them
  • Interview:
    • Practice Neetcode
    • Just like anything techique based, aim to think in principles and practice very directly for this
    • Take adderall for this if you need to, this sort of prep is always bitter medicine and it fucking sucks
    • Use Claude as your tutor to hammer in the right way of thinking
  • Connections & Soft Skills
    • Ask people for things. Don't be afraid to, really hammer in that the worst they say is no and if you ask politely they will feel compelled to help because that's how human brains work.
    • If you can't do that, you are gigafucked.
    • And if they say no because you're too cringe to be around and they just don't want to hang out with you, you can either fix this by becoming better – or if that doesn't work, well c.f. Frank Yang's advice on getting rejected by girls
    • Be nice I guess
    • Be compelling
    • Be authentic – this is terrible advice for many people because their authentic self is lame. But it's good advice for me for a reason I have no idea why. It's more that I can't really be inauthentic, as a sort of limitation of my entire being. Whatever.

Everything is terrible

The job market is terrible. CS just makes me want to kill myself, everything else does too. Life is a long, hard slog until inevitable demise. And we have AGI coming to spice things up for the worse (or better, but this is the real world, so probably worse). Bad things just happen. Bad things are allowed to happen, like fucking getting cancer and dying. This happens.

What's going to happen is we will finish assembling the proto-AGI "automated AI researcher" required for an intelligence explosion a la Carl Shulman, giving us – immediately right after that because you can run 800,000 copies of that thing immediately for researcher – ASI. Then, I don't know man. That's what's going to happen man, and it's like pretty fucked nightmare stuff.

But if you try to delude yourself into enjoying what you hate, that will lead to self-resentment. You will take this out in some unhealthy outlet and hurt yourself just to normalize your constant bad feelings. Better to admit it is how it is than clench your teeth.

There's some value in having a regular activity you like, e.g. for me that's climbing, lifting, etc – but that doesn't always work. Maybe you can go on a fun adventure with friends, maybe playing paintball will help. I'm not sure. Maybe like, go have sex or something. The more simmering resentment there is, the less those things work.

I don't know what the solution is. I have written about this before but I think I'm not satisfied with my answer anymore. bruh
