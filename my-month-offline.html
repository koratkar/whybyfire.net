2026-03-02 – 2026-??-?? • status: in-progress •

This page is a record of my digital declutter conducted from March 2nd to April 2nd. My chronic use of the internet appears to have psychological effects such as low motivation and a grealty increased task initiation threshold. As of March 2nd, I'm behind on my homework by weeks and have missed several tests. I also see these as greatly negatively impacting my life, despite the value I gain from my online relationships and communities. Since I have met many of the people I interact with online, I don't really see the internet as purely parasocial. I strongly reject the idea that discord and twitter friendships are not true socialization, but I do realize that using social media as much as I do confers more harm than good. I don't know where to draw the limit so in the spirit of Cal Newport's "digital declutter" and elimination diets, I will cut everything out for a month.

My internet restriction started on March 2nd, 2026 and lasts until April 2nd, 2026. On April 2nd, I will start carefully reintroducing things to my life and seeing how they affect me.

I am recording each day of the declutter to see how eliminating the ambient digital distractions from my life improves. I've occasionally seen remarkable improvement with two-day long "digital fasts" but have lacked the discipline to enforce them for a longer period of time. I frequently slip-up and lose interest in bearing out the first few days of boredom. In order to combat that this time, I am making a $20:$0 bet with a friend on whether I can ride out the month (e.g. if I slip-up, I pay $20, but if I don't, they don't pay anything).

Restricted Activities and My Alternatives

During a "digital declutter", Cal Newport advises creating a list of restricted activities and pairing that with a list of alternatives to intentionally use instead. In full honesty, I have trouble revisiting Cal Newport's work because I dislike his writing style and find his recent writing on AI to be borderline sophistic and occasionally even incorrect, but his ideas on technology use are often good regardless.
