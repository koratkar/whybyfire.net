My Month Offline

personal record
2026-03-02 – 2026-??-?? • status: in-progress

This page is a record of my digital declutter conducted from March 2nd to April 2nd. My chronic use of the internet appears to have psychological effects such as low motivation, a grealty increased task initiation threshold causing procrastination, and disrupted sleep. As of March 2nd, I'm behind on my homework by weeks and have missed several tests. My bedtime ranges between 1AM and 4AM. This is unsustainable and greatly negatively impacts my life despite the value I gain from my online relationships and communities. Since I have met in-person many of the people I interact with online, I don't really see the internet as purely parasocial. I strongly reject the idea that discord and twitter friendships are not true socialization, but I do realize that using social media as much as I do confers more harm than good. I don't know where to draw the limit so in the spirit of Cal Newport's "digital declutter" and elimination diets, I will cut everything out for a month.

My internet restriction started on March 2nd, 2026 and lasts until April 2nd, 2026. On April 2nd, I will start carefully reintroducing things to my life and seeing how they affect me.

I am recording each day of the declutter to see how eliminating the ambient digital distractions from my life improves. I've occasionally seen remarkable improvement with two-day long "digital fasts" but have lacked the discipline to enforce them for a longer period of time. I frequently slip-up and lose interest in bearing out the first few days of boredom. In order to combat that this time, I am making a $20:$0 bet with a friend on whether I can ride out the month (e.g. if I slip-up, I pay $20, but if I don't, they don't pay anything).

Restricted Activities and My Alternatives

During a digital declutter, Cal Newport advises creating a list of restricted activities and pairing that with a list of alternatives to intentionally use instead. In full honesty, I have trouble revisiting Cal Newport's work because I dislike his writing style and find his recent writing on AI to be borderline sophistic and occasionally even incorrect, but his ideas on technology use and the value of uninterrupted focus are often good and original regardless. Sometimes detailed elaborations on common sense ideas, like much of Newport's work is, can be tremendously valuable to aiding implementation. I owe many of my accomplishments to carefully following Newport's ideas.

The following is a list of things I'm going to be cutting out or severely restricting:

  • YouTube
  • Reddit
  • Twitter
  • Discord (except for 15 minutes on Sunday and Wednesday to check information for IRL meetups I attend)
  • Podcasts
  • Music
  • Online meetups
  • My phone
  • Hacker News, Substack

Since I sometimes need YouTube and Reddit when searching for things, I am only barring them as recreational activities. Using them to fill silence and using them intentionally to find information are completely separate in my experience. I am blocking reddit on all devices and only accessing it when necessary via the internet archive.

Since I need my phone for maps and communication sometimes, I'm going to leave it at home in its lockbox for a month, using a physical keyboard connected via USB to type if necessary. I am switching to an old flip phone I will carry around at school instead to take calls in urgent situations.

Broadly, I aim to eliminate the internet as a space for recreation or ambiently filling silent or bored moments, which means even cutting out activities like aimlessly browsing Wikipedia or Googling things for fun, though it would be too much to cut out Wikipedia or Google as services.

The following is a list of things I'm going to substitute them with:

  • Staring at a wall and going for walks in place of using the internet as a distraction to quell my negative emotions
  • Reading books in place of the internet to fill silent moments
  • Homework in place of obsessively checking the news to keep track of things
  • My different fitness hobbies, e.g. climbing, lifting, running in place of aimless online recreation
  • Applying for jobs in place of chatting on discord
  • Clubs (climbing, philosophy club) in place of socializing on discord
  • Meeting friends in place of socializing on discord or twitter
  • Flip phone in place of phone

Daily Log

March 2nd

5:15PM – Wrote my list of restrictions, told online friends I would be offline for accountability, logged out of all social media accounts, and made the $20 enforcement agreement with friend. My month offline has begun. I still feel a little bit like social media is "accessible" through the internet because I haven't added youtube to my website blocker. I want to feel offline in a way I'm not sure this rule allows. I will see how I feel in a bit. Having so much access to the internet feels like leaving a million tabs open in my brain, and creating new restrictions is like force quitting the whole application. It might take some time to fully close those loops. Aside from that small gap in my rule set, I feel quite confident about this project overall given the harshness of the agreement I made and severity for failure.
